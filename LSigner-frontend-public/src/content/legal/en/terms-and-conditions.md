## 2. Service description

LSigner allows its Users to sign documents electronically thru the following process:

1. Login using multi-factor authentication (MFA).
2. Additional verification using a one-time code (OTP) sent to the registered email, generated and fully validated by LSigner systems. Additional verification thru a one-time password (OTP) sent to the registered email, generated and fully validated by LSigner systems.
3. Once the identity is verified, LSigner systems apply a cryptographic signature to the document and generate a signed PDF file, along with an evidence record associated with that operation (see [Electronic Signature Policy](/legal/electronic-signature-policy)).

As of the date of these Terms, the signature generated by LSigner is classified as a **Simple Electronic Signature (SES)** in accordance with Regulation (EU) No. 910/2014 (eIDAS), reinforced by multiple authentication factors and a comprehensive record of technical evidence. LSigner expressly informs that, unlike an Advanced Electronic Signature (AES) or Qualified Electronic Signature (QES), the SES does not enjoy a legal presumption of enhanced reliability, although it produces legal effects and is admissible as evidence in judicial proceedings, being evaluated according to the general rules of electronic evidence.

LSigner may, in the future, offer higher signature levels (AES) as its technical infrastructure evolves, which will be communicated to Users in a timely manner and reflected in the corresponding update of these Terms and the Electronic Signature Policy.
